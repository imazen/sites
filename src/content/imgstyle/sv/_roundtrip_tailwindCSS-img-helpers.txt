TailwindCSS provides several helper classes for working with the `<img>` tag. Here are some of the most commonly used ones:
- `object-contain`: This class sets the image to fit within its container while maintaining its aspect ratio. It shrinks or expands the image as necessary to ensure that it fits within the container.
- `object-cover`: This class sets the image to cover the entire container while maintaining its aspect ratio. It crops or shrinks the image as necessary to ensure that it covers the container.
- `object-fill`: This class sets the image to fill the entire container, without maintaining its aspect ratio. This can cause the image to stretch or compress to fit within the container.
- `object-none`: This class sets the image to its natural size without resizing or cropping it.
- `object-center`: This class horizontally and vertically centers the image within its container.
- `object-left`: This class places the image to the left within its container.
- `object-right`: This class places the image to the right within its container.
- `object-top`: This class places the image at the top of its container.
- `object-bottom`: This class places the image at the bottom of its container.

These classes can be used in combination with other TailwindCSS classes to further style your images, such as setting a specific width or height, adding borders or shadows, or adjusting opacity.
