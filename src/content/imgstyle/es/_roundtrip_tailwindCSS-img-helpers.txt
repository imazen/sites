TailwindCSS provides several auxiliary classes for working with the `<img>` tag. Here are some of the most commonly used:

- `object-contain`: This class adjusts the image to fit inside its container while maintaining its aspect ratio. The image will be scaled down or up as needed to ensure it fits inside the container.
- `object-cover`: This class adjusts the image to cover the entire container while maintaining its aspect ratio. The image will be cropped or scaled down as needed to ensure it covers the entire container.
- `object-fill`: This class adjusts the image to fill the entire container without maintaining its aspect ratio. This can cause the image to stretch or compress to fit inside the container.
- `object-none`: This class adjusts the image to its natural size, without any resizing or cropping.
- `object-center`: This class centers the image horizontally and vertically inside its container.
- `object-left`: This class aligns the image to the left inside its container.
- `object-right`: This class aligns the image to the right inside its container.
- `object-top`: This class aligns the image to the top of its container.
- `object-bottom`: This class aligns the image to the bottom of its container.

These classes can be used in conjunction with other TailwindCSS classes to style your images, such as setting a specific width or height, adding borders or shadows, or adjusting opacity.
